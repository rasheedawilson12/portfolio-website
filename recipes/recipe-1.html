Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowls

with Edamame Salad, Pickled Cucumber & Spicy Soy Mayo

Do some bowls leave you feeling ho-hum? Prepare yourself for oh-yum! Rich, flaky salmon gets a triple teriyaki treatment thanks to a hoisin-soy marinade, followed by a sweet soy glaze roast, and a final drizzle of spicy soy mayo. Salmon is served over a bed of jasmine rice, along with some fresh sides: a fresh edamame and carrots salad, plus tangy pickled cukes. With a sprinkle of sesame seeds, this dish will have ya saying goodbye to boring bowls!

Ingredients
(Serves 2)

  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon horseradish paste
  • 5 tablespoons white wine vineger

  • 1.5 ounces sesame dressing
  • 4 ounces edamame
  • 4 ounces shredded carrots
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

  • Pepper
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  • Step 1
    • Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 425 degrees. Wash and dry produce.
    • Reserve 1 tsp soy sauce in a small bowl.
    • Pat salmon* dry with paper towels and place in a zip-close bag; add hoisin sauce, garlic powder, and remaining soy sauce; seal to close.
    • Shake until salmon is evenly coated.
    • Set aside to marinate for 10 minutes.
  • Step 2
    • Meanwhile, combine rice, ¾ cup water and a big pinch of salt in a small pot. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to a low simmer.
    • Cook until rice is tender, 15-18 minutes.
    • Keep covered until ready to serve.
  • Step 3
    • While rice cooks, trim and halve cucumber lengthwise. Place under a plate; press down until cucumber halves break open. Cut crosswise into 1-inch pieces.
    • Peel and grate ginger until you have ½ tsp.
    • To bowl with reserved soy sauce, add mayonnaise and as much horseradish as you like; stir to combine.
  • Step 4
    • In a second small, microwave-safe bowl, combine cucumber, 1 TBSP vinegar, 1 tsp water, ½ tsp sugar, a big pinch of salt, and pepper.
    • Microwave for 30 seconds.
    • Set aside to pickle, tossing occasionally.
  • Step 5
    • Line a baking sheet with foil and lightly oil.
    • Once salmon has marinated 10 minutes, place salmon on prepared sheet and brush with any excess marinade.
    • Roast on top rack until salmon is almost cooked through, 8 minutes.
    • Remove sheet from oven and brush salmon with half the sweet soy glaze (all for 4 servings).
    • Return to oven; roast until glaze is tacky and salmon is cooked through, 2-4 minutes more.
  • Step 1
    • While salmon roasts, in a medium bowl, combine sesame dressing, ginger, and ½ tsp vinegar.
    • Stir in edamame and carrots until coated.
  • Step 7
    • Fluff rice with a fork; stir in ½ tsp sugar and remaining vinegar to taste. Season with salt to taste.
    • Divide rice between bowls. Top with salmon, edamame salad, and pickled cucumber in separate sections.
    • Sprinkle bowls with sesame seeds and drizzle with spicy soy mayo.
    • Serve.

    *Salmon is fully cooked when internal temperature reaches 145°.
