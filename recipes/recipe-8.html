Sesame Hoisin Chicken Legs

with Garlic Scallion Rice & Roasted Broccoli

When it comes to chicken, we’re always looking for two things: golden, crackly skin and juicy, flavorful meat. Oh, you too? Enter: these game-changing roasted chicken legs. They’re all that and then some, the “some” being a dynamic hoisin-ginger-lime glaze that really takes ’em above and beyond. On the side, there’s garlic scallion rice and sesame-roasted broccoli. What are you waiting for?!

    Total Time

    Difficulty

    40 min

    Easy

Ingredients
(Serves 2)

  • 16 ounces chicken legs
  • 2 garlic gloves
  • 8 ounces broccoli florets
  • 1 thumb of ginger
  • 1/2 cup jasmine rice
  • 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  • Step 1
    • Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 450 degrees.
    • Pat chicken dry with paper towels; rub with oil and season all over with salt and pepper.
    • Place skin sides up on one side of a baking sheet.
    • Bake on top rack for 15 minutes (we’ll add more to the sheet then).
    • TIP: Line your baking sheet with foil first for easy clean up.
  • Step 2
    • While chicken bakes, wash and dry all produce.
    • Mince or grate garlic.
    • Cut broccoli florets into bite-size pieces, if necessary.
    • Trim and thinly slice scallions, separating whites from greens.
    • Peel and mince or grate ginger.
    • Quarter lime.
  • Step 3
    • Meanwhile, melt 1 TBSP butter in a small pot.
    • Add garlic and scallion whites; cook until fragrant, 1 minute.
    • Add rice, ¾ cup water (1½ cups for 4 servings), and a big pinch of salt.
    • Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to a low simmer. Cook until rice is tender, 15-18 minutes.
    • Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.
  • Step 4
    • Once chicken has baked 15 minutes, remove baking sheet from oven.
    • Toss broccoli on empty side of same sheet with sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
    • Return to oven until broccoli is tender and chicken is cooked through, 12-15 minutes more.
    • Remove broccoli from sheet; tent with foil to keep warm.
  • Step 5
    • Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine hoisin, ginger, and lime juice to taste.
    • Once chicken is cooked through, remove from oven and brush with half the glaze (save the rest for serving).
    • Bake until glaze is tacky and has dried out a bit, 3-5 minutes.
  • Step 1
    • Fluff rice with a fork; stir in 1 TBSP butter.
    • Divide rice, chicken, and broccoli between plates.
    • Sprinkle chicken and broccoli with sesame seeds (you may have some left over).
    • Garnish with scallion greens. Serve with remaining glaze and remaining lime wedges on the side.
